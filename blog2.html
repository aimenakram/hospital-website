Intermittent Fasting

Is missing meal a terrible idea or a weight-loss hidden weapon? Intermittent fasting is one diet craze that isn't going to disappear anytime soon. That is when you consciously refrain from eating or drinking anything else besides water for a set period of time. Some people fast for religious reasons, whereas others do fast to lose weight or for other health concerns.

Is Intermittent fasting, however, a good strategy to reduce weight? But how should you go about it? Is it really secure? The Intermittent fasting is basically a dietary pattern that rotates through fasting then eating in a certain cyclic manner on a routine basis. Most diet practices focus on what foods to consume, the intermittent fasting concentrates about the timings like when to take food.

One eats at specific timings while practicing the intermittent fasting. To fast for the specific no. of hours/day or to eat one meal few days per week, can assist body burn fat. Recent researches also indicate certain health advantages of it.

The Intermittent fasting can be practiced in a number of ways, among which require dividing the week or a day into the eating or fasting time frames. You eat extremely little or not anything whatsoever during the fasting times. The following are the most often used methods:

• 16/8 Method: It is also known as the Leangains technique, and it entails missing breakfast and limiting your day eating window to eight (8) hours. It involves fasting for 16 hours in a periodic manner.

• Eat-Stop-Eat: This entails fasting over 24 hrs. either once or twice a week, such as not eating from breakfast one day to breakfast the following day.

• 5:2 Diet: This strategy involves consuming 500-600 kcals on 2 non - consecutive weekdays while eating regularly on the other five days a week.

Most individuals believe that the 16/8 technique is the easiest, more consistent, and simplest to follow.

The major Health Advantages of the Intermittent fasting are Weight Loss, Improve Insulin Resistance, cure Inflammation, Better Heart Health, Prevent Cancers, Improve Brain Health, Anti-aging etc

Apart from all the above-mentioned health benefits of Intermittent Fasting, it is not for everyone, if a person has been under-weight or has history of any other disorder, it should never be practiced without the consultation of a Dietitian or Nutritionist. Having said that, intermittent fasting has a great safety record. It must not be done for longer period of time to prevent your body from long term nutrient deficiencies. As everything has limits, one must know when to stop.
